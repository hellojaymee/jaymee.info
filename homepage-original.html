What do I do?

I make the web. I spend my whole day, almost every day, experimenting with code and inhaling new information on trends and standards. I’m currently building websites for an inspiring B Corporation called Firespring. I work in the Front-End Development department where I build website after website. I work on both custom sites for individual clients as well as robust themes meant to accomodate hundreds of clients. Each design is integrated into Firespring’s very own CMS.

How do I work?

My duty as a designer is to carefully observe and properly analyze each project in order to thoroughly understand the needs of a client. Listening and planning strategically makes it possible create beautiful, meaningful designs that will exceed the needs of a creative breif. That being said - I don’t believe that all design is visual. A primary ambition of mine is to write functional, clean, light-weight code that performs as intended in all relevant browsers without fail. My duty as a developer is to strategically review every aspect of a design before coding. I functionality and foundational plan out how a site will be coded before I even open up Sublime Text.
